Lafeyette Coney Island

A Detroit original, Lafeyette Coney Island is king in the Motor City. It's most notable item is the Coney Island Hot Dog. Consisting of a natural casing hot dog, steamed bun, beanless chill, onion, and mustard, it's a quintessential item on any diner menu. While it is well known in the Metro-Detroit area, the hot dog itself refers to the hot dogs served at Coney Island, but the preparation refers to the restaurant it hails from.

By Phil Valenti

The Cadieux Cafe

Steamed mussels and feather bowling, it's unique experience in it's own. Since the 1960s, it's been a hotspot for entertainment. What's feather bowling you ask? The object of the game is to bowl a cheese-wheel shaped, wooden ball down an alley towards a feather sticking up out of the ground. Side note, the Cadieux Cafe is one of two locations in Michigan that has a feather bowling alley.

By Phil Valenti
